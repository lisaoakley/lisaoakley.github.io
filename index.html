About Me

I am a recent graduate from Northeastern University with a BS in Mathematics and Computer Science.
Currently, I am working as a Research Assistant for Dr. Alina Oprea in the Northeastern University Cybersecurity and Privacy Institute with an concentration on Adversarial Machine Learning.
My professional interests are in cloud computing and cryptography, with strong interest in theoretical and mathematical topics. I read The Code Book by Simon Singh to learn more about the history of cryptography and cryptanalysis. I have also been making my way slowly through Godel, Escher, Bach: an Eternal Golden Braid by Douglas Hofstadter, and have been enjoying his style of explaining how machines can mimic the human mind through a lens of theoretical mathematics and neurology. Additionally I am reading Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction by Sutton and Barto as part of my research.
Apart from CS, I enjoy performing and watching improv comedy, attempting to complete crossword puzzles, watching Parks and Rec, dancing, doing yoga, and running. I recently joined ImprovBoston's All Access Improv cast for the January-June 2019 season.
