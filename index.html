About Me

I am a Math and Computer Science undergraduate at Northeastern University. I expect to graduate in December of 2018.
Professionally, I am interested in mathematically rigorous areas of computer science. My goal for the next few years is to learn more about machine learning and cryptography.
Recently I started reading The Code Book by Simon Singh to learn more about the history of cryptography and cryptanalysis. I have also been making my way slowly through Godel, Escher, Bach: an Eternal Golden Braid by Douglas Hofstadter, and have been enjoying his style of explaining how machines can mimic the human mind through a lens of theoretical mathematics. Additionally I am watching lectures on Machine Learning from both MIT and Stanford and reading a Linear Algebra book. I am excited to continue both my engineering skills as well as my mathematical understanding through the next two semesters of my schooling and my final co-op and internship.
My more immediate goals are to improve my development skills and get more hands-on experience with code through my next co-op at Pivotal, outside projects, and the remainder of my coursework to help facilitate my long-term plans. In the past year I have attended an office hackathon, a security capture-the-flag event, a women in math conference, have participated in weekly meetings for both the Northeastern Women in Technology and Math Club (serving as Vice-President of the latter). I hope to get a scholarship to attend the Grace Hopper conference in October.
Apart from CS, I enjoy performing and watching improv comedy, attempting to complete crossword puzzles, watching Parks and Rec, dancing, doing yoga, and running.
